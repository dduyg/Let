‚öûDiffusion models are a class of generative models that simulate the gradual process of adding noise to data and then learn to reverse this process to generate new, high-quality data samples. Beyond generation, diffusion models are being explored for tasks such as data augmentation, anomaly detection, and predictive modeling, making them versatile tools in modern data science.
